About Me



What can I say about Motherhood?

Motherhood has been one of the most exhausting but yet rewarding journey I have ever embarked on. I have learnt so much during this short period, looking at the fact my daughter is just a few months old and haven’t reached her prime years as yet. I have received a lot of support from mothers, friends, relatives and colleagues and it’s a journey that I will be forever learning, because it holds a lot of different challenges that I am yet to experience. However, I am here to share my experiences and journey that I have faced so far and I truly believe the challenges that I have been through will really help and support newly mums, even though it’s a stage in your life that requires you to always learn and face new challenges.

I was very reluctant on sharing my story; battling fears and doubts on whether if I was even doing a great job of being a mum and obviously knowing how much a mother’s care can really impacts a childhood. I have also researched and asked other mums to also share their experiences, because every mum have a story to share that can help with ways mine one probably wouldn’t.

My motherhood journey started off with me being very sceptical to how I was being seen as a mother and whether or not I projected myself to be a good one. There’s a saying that said we cannot run away from who we really are and that also comes with motherhood, we know whether or not we’re being a good mother. We know the moments that we wish we could spend a bit more time in the shower, the hair salon or go on a shopping spree, but in life with everything that we gain also comes along sacrifices, we have to give and take and motherhood is all about that; being selfless and remembering we have someone that is depending on us for everything. One of most regarding moments of being a mother is knowing that you’re needed, you’re the life support of a beautiful being, that knows you by smell, touch and voice, a being that loves you more than anything else in this world and smiles at you whenever you’re in their sight and cries when you’re not.

Let me go to the stage before I was able to hold this beautiful gift from God in my arms, the stage where she was developing in my stomach and my experiences; the stage of being pregnant. During pregnancy I didn’t face as much struggles as I thought I would, I didn’t really experience morning sickness, but one of the hardest moments I must say was when I lost my brother which was at the beginning of my pregnancy. This stage in my life made me realised that not only will I be facing the challenge of being a new mum but I need to be ready for the challenges that will come from the outside regarding the different areas in my life. This moment is where I had to be strong as a person both for my unborn baby as well as my family and yes it was hard, but I had to learn how to handle it. I was also going through a lot of changes in terms of my body; my sense of smell, I was very sensitive to certain perfumes, which made me feel sick and my sense of taste, I diverted from food that I would previously loved to eat to disliking them.

My journeys to work was also very challenging, especially travelling underground which will get really hot at times and the only thing that would cool me down would be a mango and passion fruit smoothie until my body started rejecting it and then I found a new love which was super-malt.
